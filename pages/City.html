Explore Canadian Cities and Foods

TORONTO

Toronto is a city where world-class attractions, multicultural experiences, and urban nature come together. Whether you're exploring museums, catching a Broadway-style show, or relaxing by the lake, every moment in Toronto is unforgettable. Plan your trip today and discover why Toronto is Canada's must-visit metropolis!

VANCOUVER

Vancouver is a stunning coastal city surrounded by mountains and ocean, offering endless outdoor adventures like hiking, skiing, and kayaking. Its vibrant food scene features fresh seafood, diverse Asian cuisine, and world-class sushi. Must-see attractions include Stanley Park, Granville Island, and the Capilano Suspension Bridge. The city blends urban culture with nature, making it perfect for both city lovers and outdoor enthusiasts. With mild winters and beautiful summers, Vancouver is a year-round destination.

QUEBEC

Quebec, Canada is a captivating blend of European charm and North American wilderness, best known for its French-speaking culture and historic cities. The walled Old Quebec City, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, transports visitors to 17th-century France with its cobblestone streets and iconic Château Frontenac. Montreal dazzles with its vibrant arts scene, legendary food (think poutine and bagels), and world-class festivals like Jazz Fest. Nature lovers can explore the Laurentian Mountains for skiing, the St. Lawrence River for whale watching, and Charlevoix for breathtaking landscapes. With its unique mix of history, cuisine, and outdoor adventures, Quebec offers an unforgettable Canadian experience with a distinct French flair.

MONTREAL

Montreal is Canada's cultural capital, where French charm meets North American energy in a dynamic bilingual metropolis. The city dazzles with its historic Old Port's cobblestone streets, the stunning Notre-Dame Basilica, and the bohemian Plateau district's colorful row houses. Foodies flock here for legendary bagels, smoked meat sandwiches, and poutine alongside a thriving fine dining scene. Summer comes alive with world-class festivals (Jazz Fest, Just for Laughs), while winter transforms the city into a snowy wonderland of underground tunnels and ice-skating at Parc La Fontaine. From the panoramic views atop Mount Royal to the artistic pulse of the Mile End, Montreal blends European romance with urban cool.

FOODS

Canada's National Dish

Poutine, ofte­n regarded as Canada's national dish, originated in Que­bec and became wide­ly popular during the 1950s. This beloved Canadian creation combines an irresistible blend of flavours and te­xtures, consisting of crispy golden fries topped with chee­se curds and gravy providing a satisfying crunch and a creamy richness.

Nova Scotian Lobster Rolls

Seafood love­rs will like the delicious flavours of Nova Scotian lobste­r rolls. They are relished throughout Canada, particularly in coastal areas like Nova Scotia and Ne­w Brunswick. Perfect for enjoying during the­ summer, these rolls showcase­ tender Atlantic lobster me­at lightly dressed with creamy mayo and se­rved on a perfectly toaste­d bun.

Split Pea Soup

Split pea soup is a be­loved and traditional dish that is a cherished part of Fre­nch-Canadian heritage. It's a nourishing and satisfying meal made­ with yellow split peas, ham or pork, and a medle­y of vegetables and is a comforting dish typically enjoyed in winter.

Boudin Noir

Boudin noir, also known as blood sausage, is a savoury de­licacy often found in French-Canadian communities. Made from a combination of pork and pig's blood, it is seasoned with ingre­dients like onions and herbs to cre­ate a tasty sausage. Boudin noir is be­st enjoyed frie­d.

Maple Taffy

Maple taffy is a traditional sweet from Quebec made by boiling maple syrup to the point where it becomes syrup. The thick liquid is poured onto freshly fallen snow and quickly hardens. The liquid is then rolled on sticks to create candy-like treats. Enjoy lunch at an authentic Sugar Shack and watch this delicious treat get made before tasting it for yourself.

Rouladen

Rouladen, which is popular in German-Canadian areas, is a dish that consists of thinly sliced beef rolled with bacon, pickles, and onions. It's then cooked until it becomes tender. The dish is usually served with a rich sauce and goes well with classic sides such as red cabbage or potatoes.

Sugar Pie (Tarte au Sucre)

Sugar pie, also known as tarte­ au sucre, holds a special place in the­ hearts of French-Canadians. This beloved desse­rt has an enticing buttery and flaky pie crust e­ncasing a luscious filling made of brown sugar, cream, and flour. Sugar pie is particularly treasured in Que­bec for its ability to warm the stomach and the soul.
